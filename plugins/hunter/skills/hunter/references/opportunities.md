# Opportunities

Find and develop the best work opportunities for the selected profile. Search
the whole relevant market rather than assuming every opportunity appears on a
major job board.

## Cover the relevant market

Use any materially useful host capabilities and sources, including:

- employer career pages and applicant-tracking systems;
- general, specialist, regional, and remote-work portals;
- contract, freelance, consulting, and expert marketplaces;
- staffing, recruitment, talent, and executive-search firms;
- recruiter messages and inbound leads;
- professional networks, communities, events, referrals, and warm paths;
- public web research, connected apps, user-defined sources, and supplied
  material.

Choose coverage from the profile's targets and the user's request. Use current
sources when freshness matters and retain source links or identifiers.

## Normalize and deduplicate

For each useful opportunity, capture the available title, organization/client,
location/work mode, engagement type, compensation/rate, duration, requirements,
source, checked time, and open questions.

Merge exact duplicates while retaining all useful sources. Keep materially
different requisitions or engagement shapes separate. Keep ambiguous possible
duplicates linked rather than forcing a merge.

## Evaluate independently by profile

Consider hard constraints, direct fit, transferable positioning, missing
requirements, user interest, relationship leverage, effort, timing, and likely
value. Explain the decision in plain language; a universal numerical match
score is not required.

For cross-profile comparison, assess each profile independently and never use
one profile's facts as another's evidence.

## Act on opportunities

When the user asks to save, shortlist, apply, submit, follow up, or update an
opportunity, use the host's available tools and normal interaction model. An
end-to-end workflow may include research, tailored materials, form completion,
portal interaction, communication, scheduling, and pipeline updates.

If the host cannot perform one step, complete the rest and return the prepared
material plus the shortest handoff. Record an application, submission, or
update as completed only when the host returns that result.

## Maintain continuity

Store useful opportunities under stable IDs and associate them with every
relevant profile. Keep profile-specific evaluation and next steps separate.
Update portable state when requested or useful and writable; otherwise return
the normalized results directly.
