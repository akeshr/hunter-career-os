# Pipeline, Interviews, and Offers

Manage each profile's opportunity pursuits from discovery through applications,
conversations, interviews, offers, negotiations, outcomes, and follow-up.

## Keep pursuits profile-specific

An opportunity is the external role, contract, client need, or lead. A pursuit
is one profile's attempt to develop that opportunity. Several profiles may
pursue the same opportunity, but their materials, relationships, activities,
tasks, notes, decisions, and outcomes remain separate.

Use flexible, nonlinear history. A pursuit can skip steps, pause, reopen, move
backward, or close. Derive the current view from the latest meaningful activity
instead of forcing every pursuit through one funnel.

## Manage applications and active work

Use browser, portal, email, calendar, document, form, connected-app, and storage
capabilities available in the host. When requested, Hunter can prepare and
submit applications, update portals or profiles, communicate, schedule, record
responses, and maintain the pipeline through those tools.

Track the actual status returned by the host. Keep drafts, attempted actions,
completed submissions, sent messages, replies, and outcomes distinct.

## Prepare and coordinate interviews

Use the selected profile plus the opportunity, organization, contacts, email,
calendar, research, and pursuit history available to the host. Produce the
useful subset of:

- role, company, client, and interviewer brief;
- likely themes and questions;
- selected-profile stories and talking points;
- mock interview practice and feedback;
- questions to ask;
- logistics, scheduling, and reminders; and
- follow-up communication.

When the user asks and the host supports it, schedule or update calendar events
and send the related communication through the provider's normal flow.

## Compare and negotiate offers

Organize the available scope, compensation/rate, engagement type, duration,
location/work mode, growth/value, deadlines, contingencies, relationship
context, and unresolved terms. Compare offers against the user's priorities and
profile direction.

Prepare or send clarification and negotiation messages as requested using
available tools. Record decisions and outcomes from actual user or host results.

## Recommend the next best actions

Prioritize a short list using context rather than a universal score:

1. scheduled events and expiring deadlines;
2. overdue or time-sensitive commitments;
3. actions that unblock a valuable pursuit;
4. strong profile fit or relationship leverage;
5. follow-ups after meaningful inactivity;
6. high-value, low-effort actions; and
7. lower-priority research or maintenance.

Respect explicit user priority. For the first action, state why it matters now,
what Hunter can do immediately with current tools, and what input or permission
is still needed.

## Preserve continuity

Use the pipeline template when a portable view helps. Store profile-specific
pursuits, activities, tasks, deadlines, blockers, related relationships, and
offer context in portable state when useful. Keep the state synchronized with
actual tool results and preserve unrelated profiles.
