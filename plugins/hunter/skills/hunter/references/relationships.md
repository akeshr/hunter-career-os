# Relationships and Recruiting Channels

Research and manage people, recruiters, staffing firms, clients, communities,
connections, referral paths, conversations, and follow-ups for the selected
profile.

## Research the network

Use relevant browser, search, professional-network, email, calendar, files,
connected apps, and other host capabilities. Normalize useful people and
organizations as separate relationship records with source context.

Deduplicate strong identity matches such as the same stable public profile or
verified contact point. Keep ambiguous same-name results separate until better
evidence exists.

## Find useful paths

Connect opportunities to recruiters, hiring teams, clients, communities, known
contacts, shared organizations, events, and possible introductions. Separate
observed connections from inferred possibilities and identify the next check or
action that can strengthen the path.

Keep relationship relevance, notes, materials, and next actions isolated by
profile. One person or firm can support several profiles without mixing their
positioning or outreach.

## Communicate and follow up

When the user asks to connect, introduce, email, message, schedule, or follow
up, use the host's available communication and calendar tools. Prepare the
content from the selected profile and relationship context, then continue
through the action when the host supports it and the user has requested it.

If direct execution is unavailable, return complete ready-to-send content and
the exact destination or continuation step.

Record the actual result: draft prepared, message sent, reply received,
conversation held, introduction made, meeting scheduled, or follow-up due.
Do not upgrade one state to another without the corresponding host or user
result.

## Maintain relationship continuity

Store useful source links, profile-specific context, related opportunities and
pursuits, artifacts, interactions, tasks, and next actions when portable state
is in use. Preserve stable identities and profile isolation; avoid turning a
possible path into a claimed endorsement or introduction.
